## Themes To Prepare

### 1. Background Fit

Several reports imply Harvey cares about fit with AI / legal / agent work, not just generic backend excellence.

Prepare a crisp answer for:
- Why Harvey?
- Why legal AI?
- Why this role/team?
- How your ML/LLM or large-scale systems background maps to their product.
- Why your past work is relevant to agentic workflows, document intelligence, RAG, trust, compliance, or enterprise users.

Risk from scrape:
- One candidate with strong systems/reliability background got good feedback but lost to a more background-matched candidate.
- Another report says HM was more interested when the project fit the team.

### 2. Project Ownership

Have one flagship project ready at Staff depth.

Cover:
- Problem and business/product impact.
- Your role and decision authority.
- Technical architecture.
- Key constraints.
- Alternatives considered.
- Tradeoffs.
- Failure modes.
- What you changed after learning from data/users.
- How you influenced other teams.
- Measured outcome.

### 3. Decision-Making And Tradeoffs

Repeatedly mentioned.

Prepare stories where you:
- Chose between speed and quality.
- Chose between simple and scalable architecture.
- Cut scope under deadline pressure.
- Reversed a technical decision after evidence changed.
- Balanced research quality with product reliability.
- Made a call with incomplete information.

### 4. Conflict And Disagreement

Explicitly reported.

Prepare stories for:
- Cross-team conflict.
- Disagreement with a peer / PM / manager.
- A stakeholder wanted the wrong thing.
- You had to align teams with different incentives.
- You handled feedback or gave hard feedback.

Use stories where the resolution shows judgment, not just politeness.

### 5. Leadership / Task Assignment

Reported in Harvey Values / HM discussion.

Prepare:
- How you break down ambiguous work.
- How you assign tasks.
- How you mentor senior vs junior engineers.
- How you create technical clarity.
- How you inspect progress without micromanaging.
- How you unblock people.

### 6. Mentorship Style

One report specifically mentions being asked about mentorship style and 1:1s.

Prepare a balanced answer:
- Regular 1:1s for growth/context.
- Ad-hoc support for urgent blockers.
- Clear expectations.
- Feedback tied to concrete examples.
- Calibrate style to person and situation.

Avoid sounding like you do not value 1:1s or structured support.

### 7. Tight Timeline

Reported in collaboration.

Prepare:
- How you triaged scope.
- How you communicated risk.
- How you protected quality.
- How you aligned stakeholders.
- What you deferred.
- What you shipped.

## Answering Style

Use concise CARL:
- Context: one or two sentences.
- Action: what you personally did.
- Result: measurable impact.
- Learning: what changed in your judgment.

For Harvey specifically:
- Tie technical decisions to product/customer outcomes.
- Show pragmatic judgment, not over-engineering.
- Show comfort with ambiguity.
- Show high agency without sounding rigid.
- Have a crisp "why this matters for legal AI" bridge.

## Red Flags To Avoid

- Overly long BQ answers in a short values round.
- Generic backend stories with no AI/product/customer relevance.
- Saying you dislike 1:1s without nuance.
- Conflict stories where the only point is that you were right.
- Deep dive answers that skip why/how/tradeoffs.
- Overcomplicated agent/LLM narratives when a focused answer is needed.
