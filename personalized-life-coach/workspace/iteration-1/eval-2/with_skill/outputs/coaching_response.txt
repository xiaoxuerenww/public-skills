## THE DISTINCTION: PRUDENCE VS. HEDGING

**Prudence** = Making a committed choice with clear-eyed awareness of the risks, and having a plan for what happens if the worst occurs.

**Hedging** = Keeping multiple options alive simultaneously to avoid fully committing to the one you actually want, which usually guarantees underperformance on all of them.

You can be prudent *and* committed:
- Commit fully to frontier labs interviews. Invest everything in the prep and performance you control.
- Accept the real risk: You might not land a frontier role this cycle. That's okay. You'll learn from it and iterate.
- Have a Plan B in your back pocket (not being actively pursued, but clear): If frontier labs don't work out by [date], you'll engage with strong big-tech options. You'll have those conversations then, when you have real data.

This is what thoughtful action looks like. You're not pretending there's no risk. You're not keeping your foot halfway in two doors. You're committing to the door you want, eyes open about the cost, with a clear fallback if it doesn't work.
